Take part in one of Rio’s top attractions: a cable car ride to the top of Morro da Urca and Sugarloaf Mountain. Discover 360-degree views of Rio’s many beaches, its downtown areas, and more. Prepare for an enchanting and unforgettable experience!

Copacabana is one of the most famous beaches in the world and known for its vibrant and lively neighborhood. Try agua de coco – coconut water straight from the coconut or stop at a quiosque (beach bar) to grab a cold beer or fresh fruit juice while soaking in the sun!

Visit a favela or samba school to watch and learn about the preparation that goes into the largest carnival in the world. The samba dance is considered one of the most valued cultural expressions to Brazil and considered the heartbeat of Rio de Janeiro’s Carnival.
